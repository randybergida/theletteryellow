On The Other Side (2018)

Driven by a continued love for analog gear and the immediacy of tape, we set out to make a record that met our ideals. We got the keys to a studio and recorded for 18 hours straight, then produced the rest of the record in our own production studios over the course of a year. This allowed us to craft the music exactly how we wanted, without the limitations of being on the clock. It is a groove and vibe-driven record we are incredibly proud to share.

Released: November 9, 2018
Musicians: Randy Bergida (Vocals/Guitar), Mike Thies (Drums/Keys/Synths), Nolan Thies (Bass)
Written by: Randy Bergida; except "Legos" and "Ella", written by Randy Bergida and Mike Thies
Produced by: The Letter Yellow
Engineering/Mixing/Mastering: Nolan Thies
Recorded at: Room 17, Blue Room, The Space, and Songwriter Factory
Artwork: Kristofer Porter
Graphic Design: Yuri Miyamoto

Watercolor Overcast (2015)

A psychedelic follow-up to our debut, Watercolor Overcast represents a landmark moment for the band. We committed to a truly analog process: 10 songs, a beloved Neve console, and a 24-track tape machine, all without ever touching a computer. Recording to tape means no fixing later; it forced us to be present and capture the "magic take" in real-time. The record was mixed "all hands on deck" at the console.

Released: July 24, 2015
Musicians: Randy Bergida (Vocals/Guitar), Mike Thies (Drums/Keys), Abe Pollack (Bass/Synth), Nolan Thies
Special Guest: Denitia Odigie (Vocals on "Summer in the City")
Written by: Randy Bergida; except "Make Good Art", written by Randy Bergida and Mike Thies and “I’m a Drifter”, written by Dolly Parton
Produced by: The Letter Yellow and Nolan Thies
Engineering/Mixing: Nolan Thies at The Bunker, Brooklyn, New York
Mastering: Alex DeTurk at Strange Weather and Masterdisk
Artwork: Ian CR Martin
Design: Yuri Miyamoto

Walking Down the Streets (2012)

Our 12-track debut, written in the winter of 2011. The record strikes a perfect pace of blues and rock—all with a modern kick, a strong beat, and lyrics that are both offbeat and universal. This Brooklyn-made album established the foundation of The Letter Yellow.

Released: September 9, 2012
Musicians: Randy Bergida (Vocals/Guitar), Mike Thies (Drums/Keys), Abe Pollack (Bass/Synth), Beck Burger (Piano/Organ 14 Bar Blues/Window)
Written by: Randy Bergida; except "I Can’t Get A" and "Window", written by Randy Bergida and Mike Thies
Engineering/Recording: Quinn McCarthy at The Creamery Studio
Mixing: Joel Hamilton at Studio G
Additional Tracking/Editing: Songwriter Factory
Mastering: Steven Berson at Total Sonic Media
Album Design: Yuri Miyamoto
